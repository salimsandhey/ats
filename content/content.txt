Section 2
Given Label: About ATS
Given Heading: Automated Testing System (ATS)
Paragraph: India’s automatic testing system (ATS) is revolutionising how vehicle fitness checks are conducted. By replacing inefficient, manual inspections with sophisticated, automated technology that evaluates the foundation of the vehicle (brakes, suspension, lights, emissions and overall condition), the ATS will certify vehicles in less than 10 minutes with near-100% accuracy, capturing all data in a transparent, secure manner using VAHAN portal. 
Due to an increase in the number of accidents on the roads as well as stricter regulations such as vehicle scrappage and emissions control, the ATS is becoming an essential part of every state’s transportation infrastructure. The most compelling reason to enter this market is the comprehensive support provided—from ARAI/ICAT-approved installation, licensing, service and operations. The system has been custom-designed for the needs of the Indian market and has been built with energy efficiency in mind.
As there are very few available licences and the demand continues to grow, entering this market at an early stage will provide you with a competitive edge and unlimited opportunities to establish long-term partnerships with transportation agencies and experience continued growth as the market grows.

Section 4
Government Policy & Compliance 
Given Main Heading: Regulatory Framework We Operate Under:
GSR 652 (E) — The foundation of ATS in India is the GSR 652 (E) rule. It establishes the need for automated testing around several areas (brakes, lights, emissions etc.) with integration into VAHAN, as well as setting an exact standard from day one for our system setups.
GSR 797 (E) — The GSR 797 (E) regulation has turned ATS into a more thorough and efficient testing solution. With better calibration and real-time data, along with the ability to test multiple lanes, you’ll see a significant improvement in your testing speeds and reliability. Through our processes, we take care of all of those technical requirements for you.
GSR 195 (E), dated 14.03.2024 — The current regulation, GSR 195 (E), requires you to have approved equipment with QR codes for tracking and full digital reporting. It also helps to support the scrappage policy and limits the amount of licences available. With the use of our systems compliant with the regulations you will not only gain easier approvals, you will begin your testing operation sooner.
Closing Line: Fully compliant and approval-ready—your ATS centre launches without delays.
